Opinionated features

Built for people who do not want their voice treated like telemetry.

01

Local transcription first

Apple Silicon handles speech-to-text on device, so raw dictation and meeting audio do not need a round trip just to become text.

02

Meeting memory with receipts

Transcripts and formatted notes are saved locally, so your searchable meeting history stays under your control instead of disappearing into someone else's retention policy.

03

macOS-native behavior

Menu bar surface, accessibility hooks, microphone capture, and ScreenCaptureKit keep it feeling like a utility, not a browser pretending to be software.

04

Cloud as an add-on

Structured summaries can use an external model, but the product still has value before that step. Privacy is the baseline, not a premium toggle.

Trust boundary

Know exactly what stays local.

Muesli is designed so the expensive, intimate part of the workflow stays on your machine. Audio capture, local transcription, transcript assembly, and storage live inside the Mac. If you enable a model for formatted notes, that is a visible, deliberate boundary.
